Riverside Bridge

For nearly five of the last seven years Riverside Bridge has been closed due to damage caused by flooding. Even before the damage the bridge had several significant shortfalls. The bridge was only one lane and lacked a pathway for safe pedestrian crossing. In addition, the bridge's size and weight restrictions meant large vehicles - like schools buses, ambulances, and fire trucks - had to bypass the bridge leading to increased travel time.

It's time to find a new home for the historic bridge and replace it with a modern structure that stands above the flood waters and allows access for pedestrians, buses, and first responders. Ozark is working in conjunction with Christian County and the Ozark Special Road district on plans for a new bridge that will take us into the next century.

Benefits

  • Safe vechicle passage
  • Two lanes
  • Pedestrian pathway
  • Avoid constant repairs
  • Reduce travel and response time

Project

The plans for the new Riverside Bridge call for expanding the bridge to two-lanes and includes a pedestrian pathway. The new structure will also be raised above the flood waters.
