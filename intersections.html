Intersection Improvements

Working with the Ozark Transportation Organization and the Missouri Department of Transportation, the city of Ozark was able to secure funding for two large transportation projects.

  • South Street will be expanded to 5 lanes from 65 HWY to 3rd Street/Selmore Road.
  • Jackson Street will be expanded to 5 lanes from HWY 65 to the NN intersection.
Both of these projects will be funded 100% by state and federal funds.

Although the street expansion projects are fully funded by state/federal contributions, the city must pay for a portion of the cost to improve the intersections at South & 3rd Streets and Jackson & NN. Expansion of these intersections is necessary in order for the city to experience the full benefit of the new 5-lane streets.

Ozark will be able to leverage revenue from the transportation tax to unlock millions of dollars in matching funds.

Benefits

  • Less congestion
  • Vechicle and pedestrian safety
  • Improved traffic flow
  • Shorter drive times
  • Matching funds available

Projects

90% ($6,930,000) of the funding for these two projects will come from state and federal funds. The city is tasked with paying the remaining 10% ($770,000). If we fail to raise the neccessary match we could lose almost $7 million in transportation funding.

South Street/3rdStreet Intersection

Estimated project cost: $4,200,000

  • Federal Funds: $1,680,000
  • State Funds: $2.100,000
  • Ozark Funds: $420,000

Jackson Street/NN Intersection

Estimated project cost: $3,500,000

  • Federal Funds: $1,400,000
  • State Funds: $1,750,000
  • Ozark Funds: $350,000
